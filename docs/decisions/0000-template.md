## Context

What's the situation that requires a decision? What forces — technical,
political, organizational, financial — are at play? Be specific. The
goal is for someone reading this in a year to understand *why this
decision needed to be made at all*.

## Decision

What did we choose to do? Be specific and prescriptive. If the decision
is "we'll use X for Y because Z," say exactly that.

## Alternatives considered

Bulleted list of options we considered and rejected. For each:

- **Option A:** what it was, what would have been good about it, why we
  rejected it.
- **Option B:** ...

The point of this section is for future-readers to understand that we
didn't sleepwalk into the chosen option — we considered alternatives
and have reasons.

## Consequences

What follows from this decision? Be honest about both the upside and
the downside. Categories to think through:

- **Positive:** What does this enable or unblock?
- **Negative:** What does this constrain, complicate, or close off?
- **Neutral:** Things that are different but not necessarily better
  or worse.
- **Reversibility:** How hard would it be to reverse this if we
  discover we were wrong?
